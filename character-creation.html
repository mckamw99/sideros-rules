Sideros / Astrolan — Player's Guide

Creating Your Character

Nine decisions. One soul woven into the world's pattern. Follow each step in order — the Loom works sequentially.

✦
Step I Before You Begin

The Concept

Who were you before the Path found you? What does the world look like from where you stand?

In Sideros, a character is not built — they are discovered. The mechanical decisions you make in the following steps will be more meaningful if you begin with a sense of who this person is before those mechanics are applied. You don't need a complete story. You need a seed.

Three questions to answer before proceeding. Write a sentence for each. These answers will guide every decision that follows.

Three Questions
What do you want?
Drives the Path
Not a goal — a want. Safety. Recognition. Understanding. Justice for a specific wrong. The restoration of something lost. To be left alone. To never be left alone again. This want will determine which Path the character has been called to, and which Calling within that Path resonates most strongly. A character without a want is a character without direction. The want doesn't have to be noble. It has to be real.
What have you lost?
Drives the Origin
Every compelling character in Sideros is defined partly by absence. A home. A person. A belief about how the world worked. A version of themselves. Loss is not backstory padding — it is the pressure that shapes what you've become. What your character has lost will inform their Origin, their bonds, and the flavor entries that make them feel like a person rather than a stat block.
What does the Loom want with you?
Drives the Fate Number
The Loom of Fate weaves every soul into the world's pattern for a reason. You don't have to know what that reason is yet — in fact, it's better if you don't. But consider: does your character feel watched? Do they have a recurring sense that things happen around them, not to them? Do they attract people who seem to need something from them? This intuition will become the Fate Number in Step V. For now, just notice it.
A Note on the World

You are creating a character for Sideros — a world in its fourth and most precarious era. The twelve gods fell. The Legendary Beasts who were those gods wander the earth. An organization called the Ashen Coil is actively working to dissolve the seals holding the worst of what the Fall released. The Kallos-Sworn exist to prevent another catastrophe. The Loom of Fate is watching. The Quint Bond prophecy is in motion.

Your character does not need to know any of this. Most people don't. What they know is their corner of the world — their city, their people, their work, their losses. The larger pattern will find them. That is what the Loom does.

Step II Cosmic Lineage

Choose Your Kindred

The cosmic lineage you were born into — what the Aether wrote into your blood before you had a say in it.

Your Kindred is not your personality. It is the Aetheric frequency your body carries — the cosmic inheritance of your lineage that shapes how you interact with the world's forces. A Wolf-Shifter with a gentle soul is still a Wolf-Shifter. A Dunmarim who has left Dunmar is still Aether-hardened. The Kindred is the instrument. You decide how it's played.

What Your Kindred Gives You
Kindred Traits
A set of passive and active abilities unique to your lineage. These are always active — they cannot be suppressed except by specific effects described in each trait.
Attribute Increases
Each Kindred increases two Resonance Attributes. These are applied after you generate your base scores in Step IV.
Vitality Die
Your Kindred sets your base Vitality Die — but your Path may override it if the Path's die is larger. Always use the higher of the two.
Kindred-Zodiac Resonance
Once you choose a zodiac sign in Step V, your Kindred and sign combination produces a unique bonus ability. This is determined automatically.

Step III The Cosmic Road

Choose Your Path

The cosmic road the Aether has called you toward. Not a job — a fundamental orientation of the soul. Your answer to "what do you want?" in Step I should point toward your Path.

Path Features
Abilities that unlock as your Resonance Depth increases. At Depth 1, you receive your first features — enough to play. More unlock at each Depth.
Core Resource
Every Path has a primary resource powering its most distinctive abilities — Aetheric Channels, Tapas Points, Wrath State uses, Lead-Scale Gauge, and others.
Vitality Die
Your Path sets a Vitality Die. Use the higher of your Kindred's die and your Path's die when calculating Vitality.
Calling (at Depth 3)
Your Path's specific direction — the particular expression of your cosmic calling. Not chosen until Depth 3, but knowing your options at Depth 1 helps you play toward it.

Step IV Cosmic Resonance

Generate Resonance Attributes

The six dimensions of how your soul aligns with cosmic forces — generated by the Loom's Gift, then shaped by your Kindred.

The Fated Scores Method
The Loom's Gift — Hybrid Generation
Roll once per Attribute · Then swap two scores
Step 1 — Roll: Roll 4d6 and drop the lowest die six times. Record each result next to a Resonance Attribute in the order rolled — Forcefulness, Swiftness, Endurance, Acuity, Attunement, Resonance.

Step 2 — Read: Before assigning, look at your six scores as they fell. This is the Loom's first suggestion for who you are.

Step 3 — Swap: You may swap exactly two scores with each other — moving one score to a different Attribute. Only one swap is permitted. This represents the soul asserting one truth about itself against fate's initial arrangement.

Step 4 — Apply Kindred increases: Add your Kindred's Attribute bonuses to the final scores.

The philosophy: The dice set the Loom's pattern. The swap is you. The Kindred bonus is blood. The final number is the soul.

Which Attribute Matters Most for Your Path
Forcefulness-primary Paths
Physical Force
Primal Jarl, Solar Guard, Oathed Vanguard (for strikes). Put your highest score here if you want to hit hard and often. Forcefulness also governs Athletics and most physical feats of strength. Primal Jarl in Wrath State adds Forcefulness to melee damage — every point matters.
Attunement-primary Paths
Cosmic Sensitivity
Rāśi-Siddha, Zodiac Priest, Ashé-Medium, Stellar Cartographer, most Omega Paths. Attunement governs Tapas Points, Perception, Insight, healing, and most Warding Resonances against mental forces. If your Path is about perceiving and responding to the world rather than imposing force on it, Attunement is your primary Attribute.
Resonance-primary Paths
Magnetic Presence
Ashé-Medium, Stellar Cartographer, Resonance Seeker, Star-Born, Scion-Pledged. Resonance governs all social Disciplines, the Fated Bond Active Resonances, and Weaving for Paths that use it as their Weaving Attribute. A high Resonance is the difference between a Resonance Seeker who gives their allies d6 Chords and one who gives d12s.
Acuity-primary Paths
Pattern Recognition
Comet-Mage, Geode-Pilot. Acuity governs Aetheric Lore, Investigation, History, Nature, and the Weaving Attribute for Paths that draw on analytical intelligence. A high Acuity Comet-Mage prepares more Formulations per Deep Attunement and has a higher Working Threshold for everything they weave.
Step V The Loom's Designation

Roll Your Fate

Three numbers the Loom assigns before you have any say. They define your cosmic position — who is drawn to you, what risks you carry, and what the universe has planned.

Fate's Mate Number
1d100
Determines Fated Bond compatibility with other characters. Numbers within margin = natural bond.
Primal Essence
1d6
1 = Alpha · 2–5 = Beta · 6 = Omega. The soul's fundamental Aetheric orientation.
Zodiac Sign
Choose
The twelve zodiac signs. Player's choice — or use the Fate Number to determine (see table below).
Fate's Mate Number — The Bond System

Roll 1d100. Record this number — it is yours permanently. It never changes except through extraordinary cosmic intervention.

Fated Bond Tiers
★ Paired Bond — within 10 points of another character. Shared Pulse, Resonant Awareness.
★★★ Triad — three characters within 7 points. Whisper Bond telepathy, Vitality transfer.
★★★★ Quad — four characters within 5 points. Full telepathy, Death Resistance.
★★★★★ Quint — five souls within 3 points. Never recorded.
Void-Touched Numbers
Numbers 13, 66, and 99 carry the Hollow Serpent's resonance. Void-Touched characters: bond abilities operate at one tier higher than their bond tier; the bond is unpredictable (d6 table each session); Stellar Cartographers feel the Hollow Serpent's frequency behind the Number when they read it.
Primal Essence
Alpha · Roll 1
Leaders, vanguards, initiators. Naturally take charge, naturally draw others into motion. Not necessarily domineering — some Alphas lead by example, some by force, some by being the one who speaks first. +2 to Resonance Discipline checks when leading a group.
Beta · Rolls 2–5
Community backbone. Most characters are Beta — adaptable, functional, the ones who actually keep things running. Not a lesser designation. The Kallos-Sworn's institutional memory is primarily Beta. +1 to all Discipline checks when assisting another character.
Omega · Roll 6
Introspective catalysts. Rare — roughly 1 in 6. Deep Aetheric sensitivity, natural access to weaving, carry the world's emotional weather in their bodies. Male Omegas can carry children when in a recognized Fated Bond with an Alpha. Weaving Attributes are treated as one tier higher for the purpose of Working Threshold calculations.
Zodiac Sign — Choose or Determine

Your zodiac sign is the cosmic force the Loom has designated you to express. It determines your Zodiac Attunement (Feat) at Depth 4, your Kindred-Zodiac Resonance ability, and which Legendary Beast will eventually recognize you as a peer.

Gear-Forged Exception
Gear-Forged do not choose their sign — it is determined by their Geode heart's calibration, decided at construction. A Gear-Forged player chooses their sign at character creation as part of deciding what their Geode was calibrated to. They receive their Zodiac Attunement at Depth 1 rather than Depth 4.

Step VI The Life Before

Choose Your Origin

The thread of your life before the Path found you — where you came from, what shaped you, what the Loom was doing with you before you knew to look.

Your Origin is not your Path. It is what came before — the experiences and relationships and choices that made you the kind of person the Path would eventually call. A Comet-Mage with a Merchant Origin brings a particular kind of pragmatism to their formulations. A Primal Jarl with a Scholarly Origin fights differently than one with a Wanderer Origin. The Origin shapes the person; the Path shapes the power.

What Your Origin Gives You
Two Mastered Disciplines
Specific Disciplines from the list below — fully Mastered from the start. Add your full Mastery Bonus to these rolls.
One Tool or Language
Either Mastery in a specific tool set (artisan's tools, thieves' tools, musical instrument, etc.) or fluency in one additional language.
Origin Ability
A specific passive or active ability unique to this Origin — something the character learned to do well enough that it carries into their adventuring life.
Flavor Profile
Four personality traits, two ideals, two bonds, and two flaws to choose from. Pick one from each list, or write your own using these as seeds.
Scholarly · Academic · Celestial
The full Origins document (sideros_origins.html) contains complete mechanics, equipment, personality flavor, ideals, bonds, flaws, and three narrative hooks for each entry. The summaries below give the essential shape of each Origin for character creation decisions.

Your Calling — the specific direction within your Path — is chosen at Depth 3, not Depth 1. This is intentional. Three Questions the Concordance Will Ask
What brings you to this place, at this moment?
Session Zero
Every character in Sideros begins somewhere specific. A city. A crossroads. A ruin. The Concordance needs to know not just who you are but how you arrived where the story begins. This is not backstory — it is the last step before the first step. Be specific. Be present. Arrive.
What do you know about the Ashen Coil?
World Awareness
Every character knows something — even if it's just rumor, even if it's wrong. The Ashen Coil's existence is an open secret in most regions. What your character has heard, believed, dismissed, or personally experienced will shape how they respond when the Coil becomes directly relevant. Most characters underestimate. Some have been touched by it. One or two may have worked for it. All of these are valid starting positions.
Who would notice if you disappeared?
Connections
Name one person, organization, or creature — real, in the world, with an opinion about your existence. This is not your backstory. This is your thread in the Loom. The Concordance will use this. It will become relevant. The Loom noticed you before you noticed the Loom — and whoever would notice your disappearance is part of the pattern that holds you in the world's weave.
On Fated Bonds with Party Members

After all players have rolled their Fate's Mate Numbers, compare them as a group. Numbers within 10 points of each other form a natural Paired Bond between those characters. The Concordance tracks whether characters acknowledge this or not — an unacknowledged bond still exists; both characters simply haven't named what they've been feeling.

The Concordance's First Look
Before the first session begins, the Concordance should review the party's combined Fate Numbers. They are looking for: natural bond clusters, any Void-Touched Numbers (13, 66, 99), any Quint Bond potential (five Numbers within 3 of each other — extraordinarily rare; if this occurs, the Concordance should consider building the campaign arc around it), and the party's combined Primal Essence distribution. A party of all Alphas has different dynamics than a mixed group. The Concordance doesn't force dynamics — they notice them and let the story find them.
You Are Ready
Your thread is in the Loom. The Loom was already weaving before you arrived — it will keep weaving after you leave. What you do between those two moments is the only part that is entirely yours. The Concordance awaits.
